Cabo da Roca

Cabo da Roca, the most westerly point of mainland Europe, is more than just a box to tick. The views are stunning and it is well worth a visit.

Jerónimos Monastery

The Jerónimos Monastery in Lisbon, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is a prime example of Manueline architecture. Built in the 16th century, it honors Portugal's maritime heritage and the Age of Discovery. The monastery houses significant monuments, including the tombs of Vasco da Gama and King Manuel I, making it a must-visit.

The Portuguese Riviera

The Portuguese Riviera, stretching from Cascais to Estoril, is known for its stunning coastline, luxurious resorts, and cultural landmarks. This scenic area offers sandy beaches, elegant palaces, and vibrant seaside towns. It’s ideal for surfing, exploring historic sites, and savouring local cuisine, making it a favourite for both relaxation and adventure.

Fátima, the Catholic Pilgrimage Site

Fátima, a renowned Catholic pilgrimage site in Portugal, is famous for the 1917 apparitions of the Virgin Mary. Visitors flock to the Sanctuary of Our Lady of Fátima, home to significant religious buildings like the Basilica of Our Lady of the Rosary. The site continues to attract millions seeking spiritual renewal.

Tomar and the Knights Templar

Tomar, a historic city in central Portugal, is famous for its association with the Knights Templar. Founded in the 12th century, the Templars established their headquarters here, building the stunning Convent of Christ. The city played a key role in Portugal's military history and remains a significant cultural site.
