Car Tours From Lisbon to Sintra

Embark on an unforgettable journey with our car tours from Lisbon to Sintra. Explore majestic palaces, lush landscapes, and hidden gems in comfort and style. Perfect for travellers seeking a personalised and memorable experience.

Sintra is only 25km from Lisbon, which makes it ideal for a day trip, or even a half-day trip if super pushed for time. There's no more efficient way to make the most of however much time you have available than with a private driver and guide. They can pick you up from your accommodation and take you straight to Sintra using the fastest route possible, so you can really make the most of your day out. Car tours from Lisbon to Sintra really are the best way to make the most of your time exploring this beautiful part o the world.

Typically, a day trip to Sintra from Lisbon will include the historic town centre of Sintra, The National Palace (Palácio Nacional de Sintra), The Pena Palace, and a visit to either Monserrate, Quinta da Regaleira or the Moorish Castle. Where there is not time to stop properly, it is sometimes possible to stop to take in the view and for photographs.

Of course it is possible to spend the full day in Sintra, and more, but most visitors prefer to move on in the afternoon to Cabo da Roca, the most westerly point of mainland Europe, Cascais, beaches, and other points of interest along the coast. You simply can't fit everything into one day so it is a question of choosing your priorities.

Top Tips for Private Car Tours from Lisbon to Sintra

Go early if you can

An early start is a great idea, particularly in Summer and at weekends. As the day goes on, the number of tourists in Sintra increases, so the earlier you get to the town the better. One of the most popular attractions is the Park and Palace of Pena. Therefore, at busier times it can make sense to make Pena Palace the first stop of the day.

Stop often

One of the best points about having your own car is you can really stop as much as you like. If you see a view you'd like to capture, and of course if it's safe to do so, your guide will be happy to pull over to let you seize the opportunity. In fact we go out of our way to show you hidden viewpoints and locations along the journey.

With an experienced and knowledgeable guide and driver, you can really pack in much more, see more and learn more about the country than any other form of transport. And there is no wasted time whatsoever.

Also, don't worry about bringing bags with you. They can always stay in the car and Portugal is a very safe country. This is another advantage of not having to carry all your bags with you on public transport or in and out of taxis.

Check out the Portuguese Riviera

Cabo da Roca

Cabo da Roca is the most westerly point of mainland Europe and hence something of a must-see for visitors to the Sintra region. It has great views of a cliff facing the Atlantic. There's also a restaurant, cafe and shop nearby.

Cascais / Estoril

Cascais can be found along the cost from Sintra, in the direction of Lisbon. This beautiful holiday town has become a fashionable suburb of Lisbon, for a well heeled crowd. It's a great place to fit in a meal if that works with your agenda.

Estoril Casino

The famous Estoril Casino is the inspiration for James Bond and the backdrop for some of his most famous casino scenes. As you will likely pass this location en route, it is possible to stop for photographs if you would like.

Carcavelos

A popular beach with locals, and a popular place to stop on the way back to Lisbon. One of Lisbon's leading surfing beaches, it also has several restaurants and bars serving great seafood as you might expect.
